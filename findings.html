ClusterOS has diagnosed 377 innovation clusters across 26 ecosystems in 57 countries. The finding that surprised us most is not what each ecosystem does differently. It is how consistently they do the same things.

Findings · Section 3 · Ecosystem-level data

Three patterns that cut across everything.

Ecosystem-level findings are properties of how regions organise — distinct from the cluster-level distribution above. They emerge only when constituent clusters are read together.

01

Coordination substitutes for decision.

In every ecosystem diagnosed, coordination infrastructure — partnership frameworks, steering committees, memoranda of understanding — grows faster than the commercial throughput it was designed to support. The more coordination, the less pressure to make exclusionary choices. Observable in Orlando (Team Orlando's 150-organisation MOU), Glasgow (Strathclyde spanning 6 of 8 clusters), and Cambridge (validation-by-committee across the research base).

02

Value exits through the coordination channels.

UKRI-funded spin-offs registering outside their funding region. Series A companies relocating to cities with better visibility. Graduates flowing to anchor employers rather than generating ecosystem-wide opportunity. The extraction signal is present in every ecosystem we have diagnosed — the question is magnitude, not existence. Glasgow's 25× extraction multiple is the UK headline; Orlando's Scale-Up Capital Gap is the US equivalent.

03

Incumbent anchors shape all pathways.

Federal agencies in Orlando. Universities in Glasgow. Theme park operators in Tourism Technology. Defence primes in Advanced Manufacturing. In each case, the anchor creates capability and demand but also concentrates innovation pathways around the anchor's own requirements. Activity outside the anchor's orbit is weakly developed.

These are properties of how ecosystems organise, not features of individual clusters. A single cluster may not exhibit all three patterns. The ecosystem always does.
