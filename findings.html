The diagnostic produces a stall and stack profile for every cluster in the corpus. When we compare profiles across all 377, structurally similar clusters surface — often in different countries, on different continents, under different political and economic systems. These resemblances are not curated. They are the output of an automated similarity calculation across all profiles in the corpus.

How the diagnostic reads a cluster.

Every cluster in the corpus is read the same way: from evidence, to behavioural stalls, to the configuration they form, to one testable leverage hypothesis. This is what the output looks like for one cluster — Greater Glasgow Life Sciences. The same shape exists for all 377.

  5. Dominant configuration
    Extraction–Narrative
    High confidence

    Value extraction events generate narrative about ecosystem success; narrative legitimises continued extraction by framing it as ecosystem contribution; uncertainty about whether extraction is harmful absorbed by the success narrative.

  6. →↓
  7. One leverage hypothesis
    If ecosystem reports (e.g., from Scottish Enterprise, Life Sciences Scotland, or GCID) separated "value created" metrics (spin-offs formed, equity raised, patents filed) from "value retained" metrics (companies headquartered in cluster, employment in cluster, procurement from cluster suppliers), it might reduce the system's ability to absorb success and uncertainty signals through aggregated success narratives without making the distinction between extraction and retention visible and testable.

Findings · Section 6 · Evidence pipeline

What it takes to read 169 clusters.

Cluster diagnostics are credible only if the evidence layer underneath them is. Below: the depth of the evidence base for the UK National Behavioural Diagnostic. AI processes evidence; AI does not create it.

5.67m
Companies House records

Indexed across 24 UK regions. The corporate-registry layer that lets us trace incorporation, ownership, and exit at entity level rather than aggregate count.

22k
UKRI grants retagged

Three retag passes against UKRI Gateway to Research, normalising funder, theme, recipient, and follow-on linkage so cross-region comparisons are not confounded by tagging drift.

19k
UKRI outcomes retagged

9,200 patents · 3,800 spin-offs · 3,500 products · 37,000+ collaborations. The downstream layer that distinguishes activity from throughput.

4
Sources cross-referenced

UKRI Gateway to Research (grants + outcomes) · DSIT Innovation Clusters · Companies House bulk data · proprietary investment data. Every claim joins back to entity IDs in at least two of these.

3
Pipeline versions

v2.1.1 initial · v2.1.3 Y-side enrichment, which changed tier assignments in 4 of 7 Glasgow clusters. Versioned diagnostics so changes are traceable, not hidden.

0
AI generation in evidence layer

AI processes evidence — categorisation, retagging, cross-referencing. AI does not create evidence. Every grant, patent, spin-off, and incorporation traces to a registry record.

The diagnostic framework is not static. Pipeline version v2.1.3 added demand-side evidence enrichment — measuring not just what the supercluster does, but what it substitutes for. Every cluster page shows its pipeline version.
