Methodology & limitations

Self-assessment basis. This report is generated from stalls identified by the submitting steward through the ClusterOS self-assessment tool. No actor interviews, programme data, capital flow records, or independent observation were used. The quality of the output is bounded by the quality of the input.

What this report can establish. The structural logic of ecosystems where the identified stall combination is active — including reinforcing mechanisms, leverage entry points, and structural comparators from the 221-cluster dataset. Stack detection, blind spot analysis, and infrastructure configuration are derived from validated structural relationships in the dataset.

What this report cannot establish. Whether the stalls are correctly identified (requires actor-level evidence). Whether extraction is occurring and at what scale (requires value flow mapping). Which specific actors hold leverage (requires anchor mapping). Whether stacks are structural or phase-specific (requires longitudinal evidence). These gaps are reasons to treat this report as a hypothesis to be tested, not a finding to be acted on without further investigation.

The 221-cluster dataset. Comparator matching and blind spot analysis draw on 221 clusters across ~45 countries. Co-occurrence rates are derived from clusters where stall identification was conducted at medium or high confidence — not from self-assessments. The dataset underpinning the analysis reflects a higher-quality evidence base than the self-assessment that generated this report.

About ClusterOS

ClusterOS is a diagnostic intelligence and coordination platform for regional innovation ecosystems. It gives ecosystem stewards the infrastructure to see what is actually happening in their ecosystem, not what was last reported. Built on a sovereign database architecture: the ecosystem's data belongs to the ecosystem, not to a platform vendor.

The ClusterOS diagnostic has processed 221 clusters across ~45 countries. The intelligence layer is built on the Model Context Protocol — every AI capability is a structured tool call against a live sovereign database, producing grounded, auditable, current intelligence.
